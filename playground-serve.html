SERVE Volleyball Club

Marketing & Content Strategy Case Study

Overview

SERVE Volleyball Club is a student-run volleyball community supporting recreational, semi-competitive, and competitive players. I led marketing strategy and content direction across multiple academic terms, working with a rotating marketing team while building a consistent, performance-driven social presence.

Timeline Winter 2024 ‚Äì Fall 2025
Role Marketing Lead
Team Changing marketing team each term
Tools Instagram Insights, Figma
Platform Instagram (Reels-first)

The Challenge

SERVE had strong in-person engagement, but its social presence wasn't fully reflecting the club's energy or competitive credibility.

Challenges included:

  • Inconsistent growth and engagement
  • Content leaning too promotional
  • No stable visual or content system
  • Knowledge loss between exec transitions

The goal wasn't just growth, but clarity, consistency, and long-term impact.

Goals

  • Grow followers and reach
  • Improve engagement consistency
  • Humanize the club brand
  • Test and validate a long-term content direction
  • Build a system future leads could inherit

Strategy

1. Human-First Content

Content shifted away from announcements toward people, personality, and moments.

Content pillars:

  • Execs and members as real people
  • Behind-the-scenes and humor
  • Event energy and candid clips
  • Competitive highlights

This made the account feel social rather than promotional.

2. Iterative Brand & Design Testing (3-Term Approach)

Term 1: Fun & Casual

Goal: attract new and beginner players

Result: strong early engagement

Issue: performance declined toward the end of term

Learning: too one-dimensional, novelty wore off

Term 2: Casual‚ÄìSemi Competitive Mix

Goal: balance approachability with skill

Result: slower start, but engagement improved by the end of term

Learning: iteration within the term mattered more than the initial direction

Insight: variation and pacing helped recover audience interest

Term 3: Competitive Focus

Goal: establish credibility and performance identity

Result: strongest overall engagement and reach

Learning: competitive positioning resonated most while still allowing lighter content

This structured testing clarified what actually worked, rather than relying on assumptions.

3. Performance-Led Iteration

Using Instagram Insights, I tracked:

  • Views per reel
  • Likes and interaction trends
  • Follower growth patterns

Content formats were adjusted in real time. Visual systems were designed and refined in Figma to keep output consistent despite team changes.

Results

Audience Growth

Followers grew from 2,115 to 3,355

~59% increase in total followers

Reach & Visibility

Average views per post increased from ~3K to ~10K

~230% increase in average views

Reels consistently reached 10K+ views from Winter 2024 through Fall 2025

Two reels went viral:

  • 500K+ views
  • 2M+ views

Engagement Quality

Likes per post stabilized from inconsistent 50‚Äì100 to 100+ consistently

Engagement became more predictable and less trend-dependent

Interactions scaled alongside reach

What Worked

  • Showing people, not just events
  • Iterating within a term, not waiting for the next one
  • Using performance data to guide creative decisions
  • Designing systems that survived team turnover

What Didn't (and Why That Helped)

  • Purely casual content lacked long-term depth
  • Early mixed positioning needed refinement
  • Mid-term pivots led to stronger end results

Failures became signals, not setbacks.

Impact Beyond Metrics

  • Stronger community connection
  • Clearer brand direction for future execs
  • Easier onboarding for new marketing team members
  • A repeatable content system instead of one-off wins

Key Takeaway

The biggest gains came from staying flexible. The ability to recognize fatigue, pivot mid-term, and double down on what worked turned experimentation into real growth. Competitive clarity, paired with human storytelling, ultimately delivered the strongest results.
