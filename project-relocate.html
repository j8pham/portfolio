The Story

Recreation, Rediscovered. Legacy portals, outdated PDFs, and calling facilities directly for basic information resulted in slow decision-making, accessibility barriers, and uncertainty about where to go or what's available.

Our research surfaced the real-life challenges residents face: legacy portals with outdated information, PDF schedules that required downloads just to see availability, and phone calls to facilities for basic information. This created friction at every step of the discovery and booking process.

Residents told us they needed clarity‚Äîavailability, accessibility details, costs, and schedule confidence. They wanted to know what was available nearby without navigating through redundant forms or downloading files.

Our goal became clear: make recreation discovery fast, accessible, and confidence-building. A platform that transforms confusion into clarity and hesitation into confidence.

To understand the real-life challenges residents face, we conducted a rigorous mixed-methods research process. This multi-layered research revealed both user-facing and municipal-facing challenges, giving us a holistic view of the recreation ecosystem.

Why These Methods

What Users Taught Us

01
"The map is hidden. I didn't know I could switch views."

Problem: Poor discoverability of a critical exploration tool. Opportunity: Make the map/list toggle prominent and intuitive for explorers to switch views instantly.

02
"Is this my schedule or a template?"

Problem: Users couldn't trust what they were seeing. Opportunity: Persistent state + clear labeling ("Your Bookings") to build confidence and eliminate ambiguity.

03
"Filters are the most useful thing‚Äîwhy are they hidden?"

Problem: Filters lived in an overflow menu, making them hard to find. Opportunity: Surface filters next to search for faster refinement and higher engagement.

How We Measured Impact

Because Relocate was a new product concept, impact was measured through moderated usability testing and comparative task analysis, informed by existing municipal workflows.

Usability Testing

Participants rated their experience across three dimensions:

  • clarity of information
  • ease of decision-making
  • confidence in completing a booking

These ratings were used to assess perceived usability and trust.

Result

Testing showed that Relocate significantly reduced friction in discovery, improved clarity around scheduling and availability, and increased user confidence when booking recreational activities.
